Busary Application Process

Attend Bursary Meeting

Be part of the Bursary Meeting where you can register and get acquainted with the application process. These meetings are held periodically to guide applicants.

Submit Your Application

Complete the application process by submitting all required documents. This includes academic transcripts and photocopies of relevant IDs.





Wait for Processing

Once the application period ends, our dedicated committee will carefully review each application. This process may take some time as we ensure fairness and accuracy in the selection.

Receive Your Bursary

Congratulations! Successful applicants will be notified of their bursary awards. The awarded funds will be disbursed to the respective educational institutions.

Tips for Securing a Bursary in Mathira Constituency

  1. Research Available Opportunities:
    Conduct thorough research on scholarships and bursaries available in Mathira Constituency. Check with local educational institutions, government programs, community organizations, and private corporations for potential opportunities.
  2. Understand Eligibility Criteria:
    Carefully read and understand the eligibility criteria for each scholarship or bursary. Ensure that you meet the qualifications, including academic, financial need, community involvement, or specific field of study.
  3. Maintain Academic Excellence:
    Maintain a high academic standard by consistently achieving good grades. Many scholarships require a minimum GPA, so strive for academic excellence to meet these criteria.
  4. Participate in Extracurricular Activities:
    Engage in extracurricular activities, community service, or sports. Active involvement in various activities demonstrates your commitment and leadership skills, which are often valued by scholarship providers.
  5. Volunteer and Community Service:
    Volunteer within the Mathira Constituency community to demonstrate your dedication to improving the local area. Many scholarships appreciate applicants who actively contribute to their community.
  6. Seek Guidance from Educational Institutions:
    Consult your school's guidance counselor or academic advisor. They often have information about local scholarships and can provide guidance on the application process.
  7. Prepare a Strong Application:
    Craft a well-structured and compelling scholarship application. Tailor your application to each scholarship's specific requirements and make sure it showcases your achievements, goals, and aspirations effectively.
  8. Write a Standout Essay:
    If an essay is required, invest time in drafting a well-written, authentic, and compelling essay. Clearly articulate your passion for education, career goals, and how the scholarship will assist you in achieving them.
  9. Letters of Recommendation:
    Request letters of recommendation from teachers, mentors, or community leaders who can speak to your character, abilities, and potential. Ensure these letters reflect your dedication and suitability for the scholarship.
  10. Meet Deadlines and Follow Instructions:
    Be punctual and submit all required documents before the specified deadlines. Ensure you've followed all instructions precisely to avoid disqualification.
